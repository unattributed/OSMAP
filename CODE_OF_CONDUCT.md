# OSMAP Code of Conduct

## Purpose

OSMAP is a security-focused, OpenBSD-first project. The work is technical, but
the collaboration standard still matters. This code of conduct exists to keep
the project reviewable, respectful, and sustainable for maintainers,
contributors, operators, and security reporters.

## Expected Behavior

Participants in this project are expected to:

- be respectful, direct, and technically honest
- assume good intent before escalating disagreement
- critique code, designs, and decisions without attacking people
- keep discussions focused on facts, tradeoffs, and project goals
- respect the security-sensitive nature of the project and handle risk reports
  carefully
- accept that maintainers may reject changes that widen complexity, trust
  boundaries, or maintenance burden without sufficient justification

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in project spaces, including issues,
pull requests, reviews, discussions, commit messages, and direct project
communication:

- harassment, stalking, intimidation, or threats
- personal attacks, insults, or deliberately demeaning language
- discriminatory or exclusionary language about identity or background
- doxxing or sharing private information without permission
- publishing exploit details, secrets, or private vulnerability information in
  public project channels without coordination
- repeated bad-faith argument, deliberate derailment, or review spam
- pressuring maintainers to accept unsafe shortcuts, unreviewable changes, or
  dependency sprawl

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to:

- the GitHub repository and related issue and pull request discussions
- project-maintained communication about OSMAP work
- private contact with maintainers when the contact is directly related to the
  project

## Reporting

To report conduct problems, email the maintainers at
`shopkeeper@unattributed.blog`.

Please include:

- a short description of what happened
- links or screenshots if relevant
- whether there is an immediate safety or harassment concern

Do not use public issues for sensitive conduct reports.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they believe is necessary to protect the
project and its participants, including:

- requesting a change in behavior
- editing, hiding, or removing content
- closing conversations that are no longer constructive
- limiting or ending a contributor's participation
- escalating to platform-level reporting when necessary

## Project-Specific Note

OSMAP is intentionally conservative. That conservatism applies to collaboration
as well as code. Maintainers may decline ideas that are technically interesting
but operationally risky, difficult to audit, or out of scope for a bounded
OpenBSD-first mail access platform.
